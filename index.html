About

I am a postdoctoral fellow at Stellenbosch University working on problems in mathematics and statistics, with a focus on statistical modeling and machine learning. I spend much of my time reading research, coding, and building models for complex data, aiming to connect theoretical ideas with practical applications. Before this, I spent several years working as a data scientist on financial and insurance data.

My interest in mathematics and probability started early and grew through years of problem solving and discussions, which led to achievements such as first place in the Iranian Statistical Student Competition and second place in the National Statistical Olympiad. That same curiosity continues to shape my work — approaching problems with a balance of rigor and exploration, using tools like R, Python, Julia, and C++ across data science, actuarial science, and statistical modeling.

Outside of work, I enjoy spending time in nature — hiking, exploring farms, and experiencing the landscapes around Stellenbosch.
