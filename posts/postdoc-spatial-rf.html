Random Forest for Spatial Data

Ongoing research

Setup & Notation

Let \(\mathcal{D} = \{(\mathbf{X}(s_i),\, Y(s_i),\, s_i)\}_{i=1}^n\) be a spatial dataset, where:

Symbol Meaning
\(s_i \in \mathbb{R}^m\) Spatial location of the \(i\)-th observation in an \(m\)-dimensional domain
\(\mathbf{X}(s_i) \in \mathbb{R}^p\) Covariate vector at location \(s_i\)
\(Y(s_i) \in \mathbb{R}\) Response variable at location \(s_i\)

The Spatial Model

Consider the traditional spatial regression model:

\[ y(s) = f\!\left(\mathbf{X}(s)\right) + \eta(s) + e \]

where the three components play distinct roles:

  • \(f(\mathbf{X}(s))\) — a deterministic function of the covariates
  • \(\eta(s)\) — spatially structured residuals, capturing dependence across locations
  • \(e\) — pure noise

Approach

In this project, we employ a random forest model to obtain estimates for both \(f(\cdot)\), the covariate effect, and \(\eta(\cdot)\), the spatially structured residual component. By embedding spatial dependence directly into the forest framework, the model can learn location-dependent patterns that purely feature-based approaches would miss.
