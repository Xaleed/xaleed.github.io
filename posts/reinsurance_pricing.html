Reinsurance Pricing Model

Mathematical Framework — Non-Proportional & Proportional Treaties
§ 1

Inflation Adjustment

Let \( r_i \) be the annual inflation rate for year \( i \), and \( d_i \) a development factor. The cumulative inflation factor bringing losses to the most recent year's value is:

\[ \text{EffectiveInflation}_i = \prod_{j=i+1}^{n} (1 + r_j) \]

Inflation- and development-adjusted loss and premium for observation \( i \):

\[ L^*_i = L_i \cdot \text{EffectiveInflation}_i \cdot d_i \qquad P^*_i = P_i \cdot \text{EffectiveInflation}_i \]
§ 2

Proportional Reinsurance — Quota Share & Surplus

\(S\)Insured sum (capital value) \(Q\)Quota share treaty limit \(\alpha\)Cedant retention ratio under quota share \(T\)Surplus treaty limit (maximum ceded capacity) \(c_{QS},\, c_S\)Commission rates for quota share and surplus treaties

Insurer (cedant) retention share:

\[ \phi_{\text{ins}}(S) = \begin{cases} \alpha & \text{if } S \leq Q \\[6pt] \dfrac{Q}{S}\,\alpha & \text{if } S > Q \end{cases} \]

Reinsurer share under quota share:

\[ \phi_{\text{re}}(S) = \begin{cases} 1 - \alpha & \text{if } S \leq Q \\[6pt] \dfrac{Q}{S}(1 - \alpha) & \text{if } S > Q \end{cases} \]

Reinsurer share under surplus treaty:

\[ \phi_{\text{surp}}(S) = \begin{cases} 0 & \text{if } S \leq Q \\[6pt] 1 - \dfrac{Q}{S} & \text{if } Q < S \leq Q + T \\[6pt] \dfrac{T}{S} & \text{if } S > Q + T \end{cases} \]

Residual (excess) share not covered by either treaty:

\[ \phi_{\text{exc}}(S) = 1 - \phi_{\text{ins}}(S) - \phi_{\text{re}}(S) - \phi_{\text{surp}}(S) \]
§ 3

Excess of Loss (XL) Layer

For a single ground-up loss \(x\), the amount ceded under a layer of \(m\) in excess of retention \(l\) is:

\[ Z(x) = \min\!\bigl(\max(x - l,\; 0),\; m\bigr) \]

The annual aggregate ceded loss with \(K\) reinstatements available is capped at \((K+1)\cdot m\):

\[ A_t = \min\!\left(\sum_{i} Z(x_{i,t}),\;\; (K+1)\cdot m\right) \]
§ 4

Burning Cost Rate

The empirical burning cost rate is the ratio of inflation-adjusted ceded losses to adjusted premium across all observation years:

\[ \text{BC} = \frac{\displaystyle\sum_{t} A_t}{\displaystyle\sum_{t} P^*_t} \]
§ 5

Loss on Line (LOL)

Loss on Line expresses the expected layer loss as a fraction of the layer limit, scaled by projected premium income (EGNPI):

\[ \text{LOL} = \frac{\text{BC} \times \text{EGNPI}}{m} \]

§ 6

Rate on Line with Reinstatements — Poisson Model

Let \(\lambda = \text{LOL}\) be the expected number of layer exhaustions per year, and \(c_k\) the reinstatement premium factor payable upon the \(k\)-th reinstatement (\(k = 0, \ldots, K-1\)).

The cost loading factor, which accounts for reinstatement premiums received by the reinsurer, is:

\[ C(\lambda, \{c_k\}) = \sum_{k=0}^{K-1} p_k \left(1 + \sum_{j=0}^{k-1} c_j\right) \;+\; \left(1 - \sum_{k=0}^{K-1} p_k\right)\left(1 + \sum_{j=0}^{K-1} c_j\right) \]

where \(p_k = e^{-\lambda}\,\dfrac{\lambda^k}{k!}\) is the Poisson probability of exactly \(k\) exhaustions.

Rate on Line and initial flat premium:

\[ \text{ROL} = \frac{\text{LOL}}{C(\lambda, \{c_k\})}, \qquad \Pi_0 = \text{ROL} \times m \]

Profit-adjusted premium at target profit margin \(\theta\):

\[ \Pi_{\text{adj}} = \text{ROL}\!\left(\frac{\lambda}{1-\theta},\;\frac{\text{LOL}}{1-\theta}\right) \times m \]
Note: The profit adjustment scales both \(\lambda\) and LOL upward by \(\tfrac{1}{1-\theta}\), which increases the expected frequency of exhaustion seen by the reinsurer and thus raises the premium to embed the required margin.
§ 7

Frequency–Severity Model

Frequency: The number of large losses in a period follows a Poisson distribution:

\[ N \sim \mathrm{Poisson}(\hat{\lambda}), \qquad \hat{\lambda} = n_{\text{policies}} \times \hat{p} \]

where \(\hat{p}\) is the empirical large-loss frequency per policy estimated from historical data.

Severity: Individual large-loss severity is modelled by a Pareto distribution fitted by maximum likelihood:

\[ X \sim \mathrm{Pareto}(\hat{\alpha},\;\hat{\sigma}) \]

The MLE estimate of the shape parameter \(\hat{\alpha}\) is:

\[ \hat{\alpha} = \frac{n}{\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^n \ln\!\left(\frac{x_i}{x_{\min}}\right)} \]

The mean of the fitted distribution (valid for \(\hat{\alpha} > 1\)):

\[ \mathbb{E}[X] = \frac{\hat{\alpha}\,\hat{\sigma}}{\hat{\alpha} - 1} \]
§ 8

Monte Carlo Premium Estimation

For simulation run \(i = 1, \ldots, N_{\text{sim}}\), draw \(N_i \sim \mathrm{Poisson}(\hat{\lambda})\) losses and compute the aggregate ceded loss:

\[ X_i = \sum_{j=1}^{N_i} Z(Y_{i,j}), \qquad Y_{i,j} \sim \mathrm{Pareto}(\hat{\alpha}, \hat{\sigma}) \]

Define the expected aggregate ceded loss up to \(k\) reinstatements and the per-reinstatement layer expectations:

\[ R_k = \mathbb{E}\!\left[\min\bigl(X,\,(k+1)m\bigr)\right] \;\approx\; \frac{1}{N_{\text{sim}}}\sum_{i=1}^{N_{\text{sim}}} \min\bigl(X_i,\,(k+1)m\bigr) \]
\[ r_k = \mathbb{E}\!\left[\min\!\bigl(\max(X - km,\,0),\,m\bigr)\right] \]

The initial premium with \(K\) paid reinstatements at uniform rate \(c\):

\[ \Pi_0 = \frac{R_K}{1 + c\;\dfrac{\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^{K-1} r_k}{m}} \]
§ 9

Confidence Interval for Premium Estimate

A \((1-\alpha)\)-level Student \(t\)-interval on the simulated aggregate loss distribution \(\{R_K^{(i)}\}\):

\[ \bar{X} \;\pm\; t_{\alpha/2,\;n-1} \cdot \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} \]

where \(\bar{X}\) and \(s\) are the sample mean and standard deviation of the \(N_{\text{sim}}\) simulated aggregate ceded losses, and \(t_{\alpha/2,\,n-1}\) is the critical value from the \(t\)-distribution with \(n-1\) degrees of freedom.
