§ 1

Role Overview

Monitored high-frequency trading data — thousands of transactions per second — to detect and prevent fraud and market manipulation in real time. § 2

Real-Time Surveillance Systems

Designed and implemented real-time surveillance systems to identify suspicious trading behaviors before execution, enabling proactive intervention rather than purely retrospective investigation.

The core challenge was operating at scale: the system had to process and flag anomalous patterns within a stream of thousands of transactions per second, with low enough latency to act before orders were executed.

Key manipulation patterns detected by the system:

  • ⚡ Spoofing — Identifying traders placing large orders with the intent to cancel before execution, artificially moving prices to benefit a pre-positioned trade.
  • 🔄 Circular / Coordinated Group Trading — Detecting clusters of accounts engaging in wash trades or coordinated buy-sell cycles to create the appearance of market activity.
  • 🚩 Other Market Abuse Strategies — Flagging layering, momentum ignition, and other patterns indicative of intentional price distortion.
§ 3

Post-Trade Analysis

Conducted post-trade analysis to uncover fraudulent activities and identify involved traders. This complemented the real-time system by enabling deeper investigation of flagged cases and building evidentiary records for regulatory action.

Note: Post-trade analysis allowed for reconstruction of trading timelines, network analysis of coordinated accounts, and statistical profiling of behavioral anomalies that are too subtle to catch in a live stream.
§ 4

Dashboards & Stakeholder Tools

Built interactive dashboards and analytical tools in Oracle BI, enabling non-technical stakeholders — including regulators and compliance officers — to monitor market behavior and investigate anomalies efficiently without requiring direct access to raw data or code.

The goal was to translate complex statistical outputs into actionable views: summary indicators, drill-down capabilities per trader or instrument, and alert queues ranked by anomaly severity.
